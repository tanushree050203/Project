ACCOUNTANT

"Accountant after 10th" provides detailed information about the process of becoming a Chartered Accountant (CA) in India after completing the 10th standard. It outlines the eligibility criteria, registration process, examination structure, and career prospects for individuals interested in pursuing a career in chartered accountancy. The document emphasizes the importance of strong academic background, dedication, and commitment required to become a successful CA. It also highlights the various stages of the CA course, including the CA Foundation Course, CA Intermediate Course, and CA Final Course, along with the practical training and examinations involved at each level. Additionally, it discusses the global recognition, job stability, growth opportunities, and entrepreneurial prospects associated with the CA qualification. It also touches upon the significance of continuous professional development, networking, and staying updated with industry trends for enhancing career opportunities and growth prospects in the field of chartered accountancy.
Eligibility criteria: The eligibility criteria to become a Chartered Accountant (CA) in India involves registering for the CA Foundation course after passing the secondary school examinations (Class-X) and appearing in the foundation exam after qualifying for the 12th. The last date for registration is December/June for the May/Nov terms of examination. Registration for the CA Foundation course is valid only for three years.
Course fees: The course fees for the CA Foundation exam are INR 1500 for Indian centers, US $325 for overseas centers (Abu Dhabi, Dubai & Muscat), and INR 2200 for the Kathmandu center.
Duration: The duration of the CA course can vary depending on the route taken:

  1. Foundation Route:The total duration for completing the CA course through the Foundation route is generally around 4.5 to 5 years, including the Article ship Training period.
  2. Direct Entry Route: The total duration for completing the CA course through the Direct Entry route is generally around 3.5 to 4 years, including the Article ship Training period.

Entrance exam: The entrance exam for becoming a Chartered Accountant (CA) in India is the CA Foundation exam. This exam is the entry-level exam on the journey of becoming a CA. The registration for the CA Foundation course can be done after finishing class 10, but the exam can be attempted only after completing level 12. The CA Foundation exam consists of four papers:
  • Paper 1: Principles and Practice of Accounting
  • Paper 2: Business Laws and Business Correspondence and Reporting
  • Paper 3: Business Mathematics and Logical Reasoning & Statistics
  • Paper 4: Business Economics and Business and Commercial Knowledge

    • The first two papers are subjective, while the latter two are objective. Each paper is worth 100 marks, making a total of 400 marks for the exam. It is mandatory to get 30% marks in each subject to qualify for the next step, but it is compulsory to score a total aggregate of 50%.
    The CA Foundation exam is the gateway to becoming a CA in India. After successfully passing this exam, candidates can proceed to the next level of the CA course.
    Salary: The average salary offered to a Chartered Accountant (CA) in India is around INR 7.98 lakh per annum, according to the latest campus placement program conducted by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI). The highest pay can reach up to INR 30 lakhs per year. Here’s a breakdown of the salary details for CAs in India:
  • Base Salary: INR 4.17 Lakh
  • Average Salary: INR 7.98 Lakh
  • Highest Salary: INR 30 Lakh
  • Bonus: INR 20,000 to INR 2.06 Lakh
  • Profit Sharing: Up to INR 5.11 Lakh

    • It’s important to note that these figures are approximate and can vary based on factors such as experience, job role, industry, location, and the size of the organization. Salary negotiations, additional certifications, and skills in high- demand areas can also influence the salary prospects for CAs in India.
