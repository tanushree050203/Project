INTERIOR DESIGN

After completing 12th grade, you can pursue a career in interior design by obtaining a Bachelor's degree in Interior Design or a related field. Here's some detailed information:

  • Eligibility Criteria: Typically, candidates must have completed their 12th grade or equivalent from a recognized board with a minimum aggregate score. Some institutes may require a background in arts or design.
  • Duration: The duration of a Bachelor's degree in Interior Design is usually 3-4 years, depending on the institution and the curriculum.
  • Career Prospects: Interior designers can work in various sectors including residential, commercial, hospitality, and retail. They can work for interior design firms, architectural firms, or become self-employed. With experience, they can advance to senior designer or project manager roles.
  • Salary: Entry-level interior designers can expect a moderate salary, but as they gain experience and expertise, their earning potential increases. Salaries vary depending on factors such as location, employer, and level of experience.
  • Entrance Exams: Some institutes may require candidates to clear entrance exams specific to their institution or conduct interviews/portfolio reviews to assess applicants' creativity and aptitude for design.
  • Course Fees: Course fees vary widely depending on the institute, location, and reputation. It's essential to research different institutions and their fee structures before applying.
Overall, interior design offers a promising career with opportunities for creativity, growth, and financial rewards. It's a field where individuals with a passion for design and attention to detail can thrive.
