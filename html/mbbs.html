MBBS

Here's some information about pursuing an MBBS degree after completing 12th grade:

  • Eligibility Criteria: Typically, to pursue MBBS after 12th grade, you need to have completed your high school education with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as compulsory subjects. Additionally, you usually need to meet specific entrance exam requirements set by the institution or country where you plan to study.
  • Duration: The duration of the MBBS course is usually around 5.5 to 6 years, including both academic study and practical training (internship).
  • Career Prospects: After completing MBBS, you can work as a general physician or choose to specialize in various fields such as cardiology, neurology, surgery, etc. There are also opportunities to work in hospitals, clinics, research institutions, or pursue further academic studies.
  • Salary: Salaries for MBBS graduates can vary widely depending on factors such as location, experience, specialization, and type of employment. Generally, starting salaries for doctors can range from moderate to high, and they tend to increase with experience and specialization.
  • Entrance Exams: In many countries, there are entrance exams specifically for admission to MBBS programs. For example, in India, the NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is a common entrance exam for MBBS admissions.
  • Course Fees: The course fees for MBBS can vary significantly depending on the institution and country where you choose to study. In some countries, such as India, government-run medical colleges may have lower fees compared to private institutions. However, private medical colleges and studying abroad can be more expensive.
It's essential to research specific institutions and countries you're interested in to get accurate and up-to-date information on eligibility criteria, fees, and entrance exams.
