FASHION DESIGN

After completing 12th grade, you can pursue a career in fashion design by enrolling in a Bachelor's degree program in Fashion Designing. Here's some information :-

  • Eligibility Criteria: Typically, you need to have completed 12th grade or its equivalent from a recognized board or institution. Some colleges may also require a certain minimum percentage in your 12th-grade exams.
  • Duration: The duration of a Bachelor's degree program in Fashion Designing is usually three to four years, depending on the curriculum and institution.
  • Career Prospects: Fashion design offers a wide range of career opportunities including fashion designing, fashion merchandising, fashion styling, textile design, fashion journalism, costume design, and more. Career prospects can vary based on individual skills, creativity, and networking.
  • Salary: Entry-level salaries in the fashion industry can vary widely depending on factors such as job role, location, employer, and individual skills. However, fashion designers can earn anywhere from $30,000 to $100,000 or more annually, with experience and reputation playing significant roles in salary potential.
  • Entrance Exams: Some institutions may require you to clear entrance exams as part of the admission process. Popular entrance exams for fashion design include NIFT (National Institute of Fashion Technology) entrance exam, NID (National Institute of Design) entrance exam, and others conducted by various fashion institutes.
  • Course Fees: The course fees for fashion design programs can vary greatly depending on the institution, location, and course duration. In India, for example, fees for a Bachelor's degree program in Fashion Designing can range from INR 1 lakh to several lakhs per annum in reputed institutes.
It's advisable to research and shortlist institutes based on factors such as accreditation, faculty expertise, infrastructure, industry connections, and alumni success stories before making a decision. Additionally, gaining practical experience through internships and projects during your studies can significantly enhance your career prospects in the fashion industry.
