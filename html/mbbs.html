MBBS

The Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) is a 5.5-year undergraduate degree program that prepares individuals to become doctors. To enroll in an MBBS program, candidates must have qualified or appeared in class 12 examinations with at least 50% marks and be at least 17 years old. The course is divided into three phases: Pre-Clinical, Para Clinical, and Clinical, covering subjects such as Anatomy, Biochemistry, Physiology, Pharmacology, and more. Admission to MBBS programs in India is primarily through the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET). Upon completion, students can pursue various career paths, including becoming doctors, medical professors, or medical legal advisors. The MBBS degree is recognized in many countries as the standard qualification for medical doctors and physicians. Additionally, there are opportunities to pursue further education and career specialization after completing the MBBS program.
Eligibility criteria: The eligibility criteria for studying MBBS include the following requirements:

  1. Candidates must have completed at least Class 12 with the essential disciplines of Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.
  2. The minimum percentage for candidates to be considered for the reserved category is 40%.
  3. Applicants must pass the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) in order to be admitted to the MBBS program.

These are the general eligibility criteria for pursuing an MBBS degree.
Duration: The duration of the MBBS course is 5.5 years. This includes both theoretical learning and practical training. After completing the 5 years of academic study, students are required to undergo a one-year compulsory internship to gain practical experience in the medical field.
Entrance exam: The entrance exam for MBBS in India is the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET). It is a common entrance exam for admission to undergraduate medical courses in various medical institutions across the country.
College fees: Regarding college fees, it provides a wide range of fees for MBBS courses, from government colleges with fees ranging from INR 5,000 to INR 20,000, to private colleges with fees ranging from INR 3,00,000 to INR 1.15 crore.
Salary: As for the median salary, it mentions median salaries ranging from INR 8.88 LPA to INR 12 LPA for different medical colleges.
