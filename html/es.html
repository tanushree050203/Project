ENVIRONMENTAL SCIENCE

Environmental science is a multidisciplinary field that deals with the study of the environment and solutions to environmental problems. Here's some information about pursuing environmental science after 12th:

  • Eligibility Criteria: To pursue a bachelor's degree in environmental science, you typically need to have completed your 12th standard or equivalent examination from a recognized board with science subjects like Biology, Chemistry, and Physics.
  • Duration: The duration of a bachelor's degree in environmental science is usually three to four years, depending on the institution and country.
  • Career Prospects: Graduates in environmental science can work in various sectors such as environmental consulting firms, government agencies, non-profit organizations, research institutions, and industries. They can work as environmental scientists, environmental engineers, conservation scientists, sustainability specialists, environmental consultants, etc.
  • Salary: The salary in the field of environmental science varies depending on factors such as job role, experience, location, and employer. Entry-level positions may offer salaries ranging from $35,000 to $60,000 annually, while experienced professionals can earn significantly higher.
  • Entrance Exams: Some institutions may require you to clear entrance exams for admission into their environmental science programs. These exams vary by country and institution. Examples include the SAT, ACT, or specific entrance exams conducted by universities.
  • Course Fees: The course fees for environmental science programs vary widely depending on the institution, country, and whether it's a public or private university. In general, fees can range from a few thousand dollars to tens of thousands of dollars per year.
It's essential to research specific universities and programs to get accurate and up-to-date information on eligibility criteria, course duration, fees, and career prospects. Additionally, gaining practical experience through internships or research projects can enhance your employability in the field of environmental science.
