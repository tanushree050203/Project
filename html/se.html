SOFTWARE ENGINEERING

Software engineering after 10th involves pursuing certificate and diploma-level courses in software engineering. These courses do not require any entrance exam qualification and are typically based on the student's performance in class 10. Students can also opt for short- term online courses that focus on software engineering fundamentals. After completing these courses, students can apply for undergraduate and postgraduate degrees, and even pursue a Ph.D. in Software Engineering. The average salary for software engineers after completing these courses is around INR 40,000. Top recruiters for software engineers include companies like Accenture, Tata Consultancy Services, Amazon, and Google. The eligibility criteria for these courses usually require students to have cleared class 10 with a minimum of 50% from a recognized board, with some institutes also requiring Computer Science as a subject. The scope of software engineering after class 10 includes job profiles such as Application Developer, System Software Developer, Programmer, Web Developer, and Database Developer, with average annual salaries ranging from INR 3,00,000 to INR 6,00,000.
Eligibility for software engineering after 10th: The eligibility criteria for software engineering courses after 10th vary depending on the specific course. For example, the Diploma in Software Development typically requires a Class 10 pass, while B.Tech Software Engineering may require a minimum of 60% in Class 12 with PCM+Entrance Exam. The duration of these courses ranges from 2 to 4 years, and some may require entrance exams for admission. The course fees also vary widely, from as low as INR 1,000 for online courses to several lakhs for top colleges.
Salary: Upon completion, candidates can pursue careers as software engineers or developers with an average starting salary of INR 3.12 LPA – 5.25 LPA, with the potential for higher salaries with experience.
