The Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) is a 3- year undergraduate course with an average annual fee ranging from ₹7,000 to ₹30,000. Some of the important subjects covered in the BSW course are Substance Abuse, Science and Technology, Social Problems, Counselling, Field Work, and Family Education. The BSW syllabus and subjects are structured to provide students with a better understanding of different aspects of social work.
On the other hand, the Master of Arts in Social Work (MA) is a 2-year postgraduate degree with an average annual fee ranging from ₹4,000 to ₹80,000. The MA in Social Work covers subjects such as Human Growth and Development, Ideology and Ethics of Social Work, Poverty Reduction, Micro Finance, and Micro Enterprises. The course aims to provide a more focused approach to societal welfare and people's assistance.
Eligibility: The eligibility criteria for the Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) course in India require aspirants to have completed their higher education, specifically 10+2 from any recognized board. It is an ideal course for those who have completed their higher education in humanities background. Additionally, students are required to have scored a minimum of 50% aggregate marks in their Secondary and Higher Secondary Education to be eligible for the BSW course. There is no specific age limit or criteria for admission to the BSW course.
Course fees : The average fees for a Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) course range from ₹7,000 to ₹30,000 per annum. The specific fee may vary depending on the college or university and the facilities provided. Duration: The Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) course has a duration of 3 years, while the Master of Arts in Social Work (MA) is a two-year postgraduate degree program.
Salary: The average salary for Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) graduates ranges from INR 2 - 5 LPA, while the average salary for Master of Arts in Social Work (MA) postgraduates is around INR 4.13 LPA. These figures can vary based on factors such as experience, skills, and the specific job role.
Career opportunities: Graduates with a Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) degree can pursue careers as program coordinators, rural development officers, social workers, professors, psychologists, teachers, lecturers, campaigners, and more. On the other hand, Master of Arts in Social Work (MA) postgraduates can explore opportunities in non-profit organizations, medical social work, HR management, counseling centers, schools, prisons, and various other sectors.
