PHARMACY

After completing 12th grade, you can pursue a Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm) program, which is typically a 4-year undergraduate degree. The eligibility criteria usually include passing the 12th standard with a background in science, particularly with subjects like Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics.

  • Duration: The B.Pharm program typically lasts for 4 years.
  • Career prospects: Upon completion, you can work as a pharmacist in various settings like community pharmacies, hospitals, pharmaceutical companies, research institutions, etc. There are also opportunities for further specialization and higher studies.
  • Salary: The salary can vary depending on factors like location, experience, and the specific sector of employment. However, entry-level pharmacists can expect a starting salary ranging from $40,000 to $60,000 annually, with potential for growth with experience and further qualifications.
  • Entrance exams: Some institutes may require you to clear entrance exams like the Graduate Pharmacy Aptitude Test (GPAT) for admission to B.Pharm programs.
  • Course fees: The course fees can vary greatly depending on the institution, location, and other factors. In general, fees for B.Pharm programs can range from moderate to relatively high, depending on the institution's reputation and facilities. It's advisable to check with specific colleges for accurate fee information.
It's also worth noting that after completing B.Pharm, you can pursue higher education options like M.Pharm (Master of Pharmacy), Pharm.D (Doctor of Pharmacy), or even opt for MBA in Pharmaceutical Management for further career advancement.
