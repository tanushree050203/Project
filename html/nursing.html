NURSING

The nursing courses available after completing 12th grade offer various options for students interested in pursuing a career in nursing. These courses include BSc Nursing, MSc Nursing, BSc Nursing Post Basic, ANM, and GNM. The average fees for these courses range from INR 1,00,000 to 10,00,000, depending on the type of course. The eligibility criteria generally require students to have passed 12th grade with an aggregate of 50%. There are also diploma and short-term nursing courses available online. Upon completion of these courses, candidates can work in hospitals, clinics, nursing homes, and healthcare settings, and can pursue various job roles such as Chief Nursing Officer, Critical Care Nurse, Nurse Educator, Registered Nurse, and Clinical Nurse Manager.
"Nursing is one of the most sought-after Paramedical specializations. Top Paramedical colleges in India offer Nursing courses that can be pursued after 12th class. Nursing courses after 12th focus on the services and processes including taking care of patients, first aid, supervising doctors, etc. BSc Nursing, MSc Nursing, BSc Nursing Post Basic, ANM, and GNM are the top Nursing courses after the 12th."
Eligibility criteria: The eligibility criteria for nursing courses after 12th grade vary depending on the level of the course. Generally, for certificate nursing courses, diploma courses, and undergraduate nursing courses, students need to have passed 12th grade from a recognized board with an aggregate of 50%. Additionally, for postgraduate courses, candidates must have completed graduation with a minimum of 50%. Here are some specific eligibility criteria for different nursing courses:

  • Certificate Nursing Courses, Diploma Courses, and UG nursing courses: Students must have passed 12th from a recognized board. In some cases, students after class 10 can also apply for various certification courses.
  • Postgraduate courses: Candidates must have completed graduation with a 50% aggregate.

    • Please note that the specific eligibility criteria may vary by institution and course, so it’s important to check the requirements of the particular program you are interested in. “The basic eligibility to pursue any nursing courses is passing class 12th from a recognized board with an aggregate of 50%. … … Further, depending on the level of nursing courses, the eligibility criteria is as follows: Nursing Certificate Courses, Diploma Courses, and UG nursing courses require all the students to have passed 12th from a recognized board. In some cases, students after class 10 can also apply for various certification … … courses. For Postgraduate courses, candidates must have completed graduation with a 50%.”
    Duration: The duration of nursing courses varies depending on the specific program and the level of study. Here are some common durations for nursing courses:
  • BSc Nursing: 4 years
  • Diploma in General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM): 3 years
  • Auxiliary Nursing and Midwifery (ANM): 2 years
  • BSc Nursing Post Basic: 2 years

    • It's important to note that these durations are general guidelines, and the actual duration of specific nursing courses may vary by institution and program.
    Entrance exam: The entrance exams for nursing courses vary depending on the institution and the specific course. Most of the top nursing colleges conduct entrance exams for admission. Some popular entrance exams conducted by different nursing colleges in India include:
  • AIIMS BSc Nursing Exam
  • PGIMER BSc/MSc Nursing Entrance Exam
  • JIPMER BSc/MSc Nursing Entrance Exam

    • These are just a few examples, and there are other entrance exams conducted by different nursing colleges across India. It's important to check with the specific colleges or universities offering nursing courses for details about their entrance exams and the application process.
