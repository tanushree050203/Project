Eligibility criteria: The eligibility requirements for pursuing a career in fashion design after 12th grade vary depending on the type of course. For undergraduate (UG) courses, candidates must gain at least 50% aggregate marks in their 12th examination. For postgraduate (PG) courses, the eligibility criteria may differ, and candidates must have completed a bachelor’s degree in a relevant field.
The duration of fashion design cou”ses also varies. For example, diploma courses typically last 1-3 years, while undergraduate programs generally span 3-4 years, and postgraduate courses typically last 2 years.

The average starting salary for a fashion designer is around INR 10,000 to INR 25,000 per month, which can increase to INR 70,000 after 2-5 years of experience.

    Career opportunities: Fashion design courses after 12th grade can lead to various career opportunities in the fashion industry. Some of the potential career paths for individuals who complete fashion design courses include:
    1. Fashion Designer:Responsible for styling different types of clothing such as jeans, jackets, women’s wear, children’s wear, men’s wear, evening wear, knitwear, sportswear, and more.
    2. Fashion Marketer:Can work for clothing companies, boutiques, or outlet chains, and is responsible for publicizing and increasing awareness of a particular brand, designer labels, or department stores.
    3. Fashion Concept Manager:Expected to think of unique themes on which a clothing or accessory range should be designed by a designer, brand, or fashion firm.
    4. Quality Controller: Sets quality standards for anything purchased for a new collection and issues guidelines for each department to ensure quality in various processes.
    5. Fashion Consultant: Helps people develop their personal style and provides fashion advice.

    Additionally, graduates can work as Fashion Marketers, Fashion Concept Managers, Fashion Consultants, and more. They can also start their own businesses and run a boutique or fashion brand.
