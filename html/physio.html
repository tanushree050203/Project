PHYSIOTHERAPY

Physiotherapy is a healthcare profession that involves treating patients with physical difficulties resulting from injury, illness, or disability. Here's an overview of pursuing physiotherapy after 10th grade:

  • Eligibility Criteria: Typically, to pursue physiotherapy after 10th, you would need to complete 10+2 with Science stream (Physics, Chemistry, and Biology) from a recognized board with a minimum aggregate score. Some institutes may also require English as a compulsory subject.
  • Duration of Course: The Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) course duration is usually 4.5 years, followed by a 6-month internship or clinical placement.
  • Career Prospects: Physiotherapy offers diverse career opportunities in hospitals, rehabilitation centers, sports clinics, private practice, and more. As a physiotherapist, you can specialize in areas such as orthopedics, neurology, sports, pediatrics, etc.
  • Salary: The salary of a physiotherapist can vary based on factors like experience, specialization, location, and type of employer. On average, entry-level physiotherapists can expect a salary ranging from $30,000 to $50,000 annually, with potential for growth with experience and expertise.
  • Entrance Exams: Some institutes may require you to clear entrance exams like NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) or institute-specific entrance exams for admission to BPT programs.
  • Course Fees: The course fees for BPT programs can vary widely depending on the institute, location, and facilities provided. It's advisable to check with individual colleges for specific fee structures.
Overall, pursuing physiotherapy after 10th can lead to a rewarding career in healthcare, helping individuals regain mobility and improve their quality of life. It's essential to research and choose reputable institutions for your education and training in this field.
