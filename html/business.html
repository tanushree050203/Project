BUSINESS COURSES

After completing 10th grade in commerce, students have various options for pursuing business-related courses. They can opt for commerce and business subjects like Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics, and more in class 11th and 12th. Additionally, there are several business management courses available after 12th grade, such as Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA), Business Management (BBM), Management Studies (BMS), and others. These courses cover subjects like Accounting, Finance, Marketing, Management, Human Resources, and Business Law, providing students with a broad understanding of business fundamentals and the skills necessary to succeed in various business settings. Upon completion of a BBA, students generally opt for an MBA, but they can also explore job opportunities in fields like banking, urban infrastructure, real estate management, business consultancies, advertising, manufacturing, and government sectors. The eligibility criteria for these courses typically include a minimum aggregate of 50% in 10+2 examinations. The average fees for these courses range from ₹10,000 to ₹3 LPA, and the average salary after completion of these courses varies depending on the specialization and job role.

    1. Attainment of at least 50% aggregate marks in Class XII in any stream from a recognized educational board or university.
    2. Clearing the entrance exam cut-offs as per the college's requirement.
    3. There is no age limit for pursuing the BBA course.
    4. Meeting the minimum eligibility of 50% in 10+2 examinations.
    5. Clearing the BBA entrance exam as per the college's requirement.

    • Entrance exam: The entrance exams for Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) courses in India include:
    1. IPU CET (Indraprastha University Common Entrance Test) Conducted by Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University in Delhi, this competitive exam requires candidates to have passed the 10+2 examination with a minimum of 55% marks from any recognized board.
    2. CUET UG (Central Universities Common Entrance Test Undergraduate):This exam is conducted for all UG courses and evaluates students on English, General Awareness, and domain-specific questions.
    3. DU JAT (Delhi University Joint Admission Test):This test is usually conducted in June or July every year for admission to various UG courses, including BBA. The paper is divided into four sections: Quantitative Ability, Reasoning and Analytical Ability, General English, and Business and General Awareness.

    These are just a few examples of the entrance exams available for BBA courses in India. Each exam assesses students’ knowledge in various subjects and has specific eligibility criteria.
