NURSING

Studying environmental science after 10th grade is possible, but most programs typically require at least a high school diploma or equivalent for admission. Here's an overview of pursuing environmental science:

  • Eligibility Criteria: After completing 10th grade, you'll need to complete your 12th grade education with a focus on science subjects such as biology, chemistry, and mathematics. Then, you can apply for undergraduate programs in environmental science.
  • Duration: A bachelor's degree in environmental science usually takes three to four years to complete, depending on the program and country.
  • Career Prospects: Environmental science offers diverse career opportunities in areas such as environmental consultancy, conservation, research, policy making, education, and sustainability. You can work in government agencies, NGOs, research institutions, corporations, or as independent consultants.
  • Salary: Salaries vary depending on factors like job role, location, experience, and sector. Entry-level positions may start around $35,000 to $50,000 annually, with higher salaries for those with more experience and advanced degrees.
  • Entrance Exams: Some universities may require you to take entrance exams like SAT, ACT, or specific exams conducted by the university or country.
  • Course Fees: Tuition fees vary widely depending on the institution, location, and program. It's essential to research specific universities for accurate fee information. Additionally, scholarships, grants, and financial aid may be available to eligible students to help cover costs.
Before making any decisions, thoroughly research different programs, their curriculum, faculty, and opportunities for internships or research projects. This will help you choose the best option based on your interests and career goals.
