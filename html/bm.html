Eligibility criteria: The eligibility criteria for business management courses after 12th typically require students to score at least 50% in their 10+2 from a recognized board. Applicants from any academic stream are generally accepted for enrollment, and all classes are conducted in English, so students must be fluent in the language. Additionally, the eligibility for a Diploma in Business Management (DBM) course usually requires the candidate to have a minimum of 45% in their 10+2, and the minimum age requirement for the course is typically 21 years.

    • Duration: The duration of business management courses after 12th varies depending on the specific program. Here are some examples:
  • BBA, BMS, BBS, BBM, BBA International Business, and BBA Finance are typically 3-year undergraduate programs.
  • BHM is a 4-year undergraduate program.
  • BBA+MBA Integrated Program is a 5-year integrated program.

    • For the Diploma in Business Management (DBM) course, the duration is generally 1 year. However, there are also online and distance learning options available, with the online course lasting 6 months to 1 year and the distance course typically lasting 6 months to 1 year as well.
    Entrance exam: For business management courses after 12th, there are several entrance exams that students can take to pursue their studies. Some of the most competitive entrance exams in India for business management courses after 12th include:
  • UPSEE (Uttar Pradesh State Entrance Examination)
  • DU JAT (Delhi University Joint Admission Test)
  • IPU CET (Indraprastha University Common Entrance Test)
  • SET (Symbiosis Entrance Test)

    • These entrance exams are essential for admission to various undergraduate business management programs. Additionally, for specific programs like the Integrated Program in Management (IPM) offered by IIMs, there are specialized entrance exams such as IPMAT (Integrated Program in Management Aptitude Test) and JIPMAT (Joint Integrated Program in Management Admission Test).
    Course fees: The average fees for business management courses in colleges can vary based on the specific program and the institution offering it. Here are some examples of average fees for different business management courses:
  • BBA: The average fees for a BBA program range from 1 to 5 lakhs INR.
  • BMS: For a BMS program, the average fees range from 8k to 2 lakhs INR.
  • BBS: The average fees for a BBS program are typically between 12k to 1 lakh INR.
  • BBM: For a BBM program, the average fees range from 10k to 3 lakhs INR.
  • BHM: The average fees for a BHM program are in the range of 3 to 10 lakhs INR.
  • BBA International Business: The average fees for this program range from 50k to 5 lakhs INR.
  • BBA Finance: For a BBA Finance program, the average fees range from 2 to 5 lakhs INR.
  • BBA Entrepreneurship: The average fees for a BBA Entrepreneurship program are typically between 5 to 5 lakhs INR.

    • It provides an overview of the average fees for some of the undergraduate business management courses. However, it’s important to note that the actual fees can vary based on the specific college or university offering the program.
    Salary: The salary for graduates of business management courses can vary based on factors such as the specific program completed, the level of education, work experience, and the industry in which they are employed. Here are some average salary ranges for graduates of business management courses:
  • BBA: The average starting salary for BBA graduates in India is around 3 to 5 lakhs per annum, and with a few years of experience, it can increase to 9 to 10 lakhs per annum.
  • BMS: Graduates of BMS programs can expect an average starting salary of around 3 to 5 lakhs per annum.
  • BBM: The average salary for BBM graduates is approximately 3 to 5 lakhs per annum.
  • BBA Finance: For graduates of BBA Finance programs, the average starting salary ranges from 2 to 5 lakhs per annum.
  • BBA Entrepreneurship: The average starting salary for BBA Entrepreneurship graduates is around 5 to 5 lakhs per annum.

    • It provides an overview of the average starting salaries for graduates of various undergraduate business management courses. However, it’s important to note that actual salaries can vary based on individual circumstances, job roles, and the specific industry in which graduates are employed.
