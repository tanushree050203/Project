ACCOUNTANT

Becoming an accountant after the 10th grade typically involves pursuing a diploma or certification course in accounting. Here's an overview :-

  • Eligibility Criteria: Completion of the 10th grade from a recognized board or equivalent.
  • Duration: The duration of diploma or certification courses in accounting typically ranges from 6 months to 1 year.
  • Career Prospects: After completing the course, you can work as an accounting assistant, bookkeeper, or junior accountant. With further education and experience, you can advance to higher positions such as senior accountant, auditor, or financial analyst.
  • Salary: Entry-level salaries for accountants vary depending on factors like location, company size, and level of education. However, it can range from $25,000 to $45,000 per year initially.
  • Entrance: Some institutes may have entrance exams for their accounting courses, while others may have direct admission based on the 10th-grade marks.
  • Course Fees: Course fees vary depending on the institute and the duration of the course. Generally, diploma or certification courses are more affordable compared to degree programs.
It's essential to research and choose a reputable institute offering accounting courses that align with your career goals and budget. Additionally, gaining practical experience through internships or part-time jobs can enhance your employability and earning potential in the field of accounting.
