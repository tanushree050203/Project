ENGINEERING

- Engineering is a popular career choice for students after completing their 12th grade. - It involves the application of scientific principles to design, build, and maintain structures, machines, systems, and processes.

  • Eligibility Criteria: To pursue engineering after 12th, students typically need to have completed their high school education with a science background, often with subjects like Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.Additionally, students may need to meet specific criteria set by the institutions they are applying to.
  • Duration: The duration of an undergraduate engineering program is typically four years, although it can vary depending on the country and the specific program.
  • Career Prospects: Engineering offers a wide range of career opportunities in various fields such as civil, mechanical, electrical, computer science, chemical, aerospace, and more. Engineers are in demand in industries such as manufacturing, construction, technology, healthcare, energy, and transportation.
  • Salary: Salaries for engineers vary greatly depending on factors such as specialization, experience, location, and industry. Generally, engineering graduates earn competitive salaries, with the potential for high earnings as they gain experience and advance in their careers.
  • Entrance Exams: In many countries, there are entrance exams that students need to clear to gain admission to engineering programs. Examples include the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) in India, the Scholastic Assessment Test (SAT) in the United States, and various other national or regional entrance exams.
  • Course Fees: Course fees for engineering programs can vary widely depending on factors such as the institution, location, and country. In some cases, there may be scholarships, grants, or financial aid available to help offset the cost of tuition and other expenses.
