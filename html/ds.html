DATA SCIENCE

After completing 12th grade, you can pursue a career in data science by enrolling in a bachelor's degree program in fields like computer science, statistics, mathematics, or data science itself. Here's some information on each aspect:

  • Eligibility Criteria: Typically, you need to have completed your 12th grade with a strong background in mathematics, statistics, and computer science.
  • Duration: A bachelor's degree in data science or related fields usually takes three to four years to complete, depending on the program and the country.
  • Career Prospects: Data science offers a wide range of career opportunities in various industries such as finance, healthcare, marketing, e-commerce, and more. Job roles include data analyst, data scientist, machine learning engineer, business analyst, etc.
  • Salary: Salaries in data science can vary significantly based on factors like job role, experience, location, and industry. However, entry-level positions typically offer competitive salaries, and as you gain experience and expertise, your earning potential increases significantly.
  • Entrance Exams: Some universities may require you to take entrance exams for admission into their data science programs. These exams could vary depending on the university and country.
  • Course Fees: The cost of pursuing a bachelor's degree in data science or related fields can vary widely depending on the institution, location, and program duration. It's essential to research and compare different universities and their fee structures.
Overall, pursuing a career in data science after 12th grade can be highly rewarding both in terms of job prospects and salary potential, given the increasing demand for data professionals in today's data-driven world.
