PHARMACY

In many countries, including India, pursuing a pharmacy course after the 10th standard is not common. Typically, pharmacy courses are pursued at the undergraduate level after completing the 12th standard with science stream (Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics).
However, some vocational or diploma courses related to pharmacy may be available after the 10th standard. These courses are usually of shorter duration (1-2 years) and may lead to job opportunities as pharmacy assistants or technicians in pharmacies or healthcare settings.

  • Eligibility criteria for such courses may vary depending on the institution offering them. Generally, a pass in the 10th standard from a recognized board is required.
  • The duration of these courses can range from 1 to 2 years, depending on the specific program and institution.
  • Career prospects after completing a pharmacy course at this level may include working as a pharmacy assistant, pharmacy technician, or in related roles in pharmacies, hospitals, or pharmaceutical companies.
  • Salaries for pharmacy assistants or technicians can vary widely depending on factors such as location, experience, and employer. However, entry-level positions may offer salaries ranging from a few thousand to tens of thousands per month, again depending on various factors.
  • Entrance exams may not be required for admission to these vocational or diploma courses after the 10th standard. However, admission may be based on merit or first-come-first-serve basis, depending on the institution.
  • Course fees for such vocational or diploma courses can also vary significantly depending on the institution and location.
