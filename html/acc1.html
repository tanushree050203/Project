ACCOUNTANT

Becoming an accountant after 12th involves pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Accounting or related field. Here's the information :-

  • Eligibility Criteria: After completing 12th grade with commerce or mathematics as main subjects, you can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Accounting.
  • Duration: Typically, a Bachelor's degree in Accounting takes 3-4 years to complete, depending on the country and the specific program.
  • Career Prospects: Accountants are in demand across various industries, including finance, government, private businesses, and non-profit organizations. Career prospects include roles such as auditor, tax accountant, financial analyst, and management accountant.
  • Salary: Entry-level salaries for accountants vary depending on factors like location, level of education, and industry. However, they generally range from moderate to competitive.
  • Entranc: Some universities or colleges may require you to pass entrance exams or meet specific academic criteria for admission into their accounting programs.
  • Course Fees: The course fees for a Bachelor's degree in Accounting vary widely depending on factors such as the institution, location, and program duration. It's essential to research and compare different universities or colleges for their fee structures.
Overall, pursuing a career in accounting after 12th can lead to a stable and rewarding profession with various opportunities for growth and advancement.
