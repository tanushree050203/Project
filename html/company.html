COMPANY SECRETARY

The Company Secretary course can be pursued after completing 12th grade. There are three stages to become a Company Secretary after 12th: Foundation Programmer, Executive Programmed, and Professional Programmed. The course trains students to handle legal aspects of a firm, and the Institute of Company Secretaries of India (ICSI) regulates and trains Company Secretaries in India. The average salary of a Company Secretary in India is 6 lakhs per annum. The course is open to students from any field, but it is specially designed for those with a commerce background. Candidates must pass the CSEET exam to seek admission to the program. Pursuing the Company Secretary course allows individuals to be trained to perform every role and responsibility of a Company Secretary, and it offers good compensation and flexible working hours. The course duration is 3 years, and candidates can apply for the course after completing their 12th standard and being above 17 years of age. Students with strong communication skills, time management, and multitasking abilities are well-suited for this course.
The Company Secretary (CS) course is a professional program regulated by the Institute of Company Secretaries of India (ICSI). It is open to students who have completed their 10+2 or an equivalent examination. The course includes three levels: Foundation, Executive, and Professional. Upon completion, students can pursue careers in corporate governance, legal and secretarial services, corporate reorganization, foreign partnerships, joint ventures, project management, financial management, investor relations, and corporate consulting services. The average annual salary for a company secretary in India is around 6 lakh. The course covers subjects like business environment, entrepreneurship, management, communication, ethics, economics, and accounting. The duration and fees for each level vary, and exams are conducted bi-annually in June and December. The profession offers various career prospects, including roles in stock exchange, company affairs departments, financial auditing firms, law boards, and government departments. The course also prepares students for independent professional practice, with responsibilities including maintaining company records, advising the board of directors, and ensuring legal compliance. The profession requires analytical skills, keen observation, and an eye for detail. The course also emphasizes the development of desirable skill sets such as effective interpersonal skills, time management, teamwork, expertise in corporate law, precise planning, and an excellent command of spoken and written English.

    • Eligibility: The eligibility criteria for the Company Secretary (CS) course are as follows:
    1. Foundation Program Eligibility:Students must have passed the 10+2 examination.
    2. Executive Program Eligibility: Students can either pass the CSEET (Company Secretary Executive Entrance Test) or have a graduation/foundation program qualification.
    3. Professional Program Eligibility:Students must have successfully completed the Executive Program.

    These are the basic eligibility requirements for the different stages of the Company Secretary course.
    Course fees: The course fees for the Company Secretary (CS) program are as follows:
    1. CS Foundation Program: The total course fee is INR 3,600.
    2. CS Executive Program: The total course fee is INR 7,000.
    3. CS Professional Program:The total course fee is INR 12,000.

    These fees cover the entire duration of each respective program.

    • Career opportunities:
  • Completing the Company Secretary (CS) course opens up various career opportunities. A Company Secretary can work in roles such as compliance officers, legal experts, and advisors to the board of directors in corporate governance. They are experts in corporate laws, capital markets, and securities laws, and are responsible for regulatory compliance within an organization. Additionally, they can work as operations managers, finance consultants, legal advisors, stockbrokers, marketing managers, investment bankers, revenue managers, audit managers, or even as chief financial officers. The average salary for a Company Secretary in India is 6 lakhs per annum, and they can work in various cities such as Mumbai, Bangalore, Pune, Chennai, and New Delhi.

    • Entrance exam:
  • The entrance exam for the Company Secretary (CS) program is known as the CSEET (Company Secretary Executive Entrance Test). This exam is conducted on a national level and is a prerequisite for admission to the CS program. The CSEET is an online, computer-based exam.
