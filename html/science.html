SCIENCE COURSES

After completing 10th grade, eligibility criteria for pursuing science typically include good grades in mathematics and science subjects. The duration of science courses varies depending on the specific program you choose, but commonly, undergraduate science degrees take three to four years to complete.
Career prospects in science are diverse and include fields such as engineering, medicine, research, biotechnology, environmental science, computer science, and more. Salaries vary greatly depending on the chosen field, level of education, experience, and location. Entry-level salaries for science graduates can range from moderate to high, with the potential for significant growth over time.
Entrance exams may be required for admission to certain science programs, such as engineering, medical, or research-based courses. Examples of such exams include JEE (Joint Entrance Examination) for engineering, NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) for medical, and various entrance exams for research institutes. Course fees for science programs vary widely depending on the institution, country, and program level. Some universities offer scholarships and financial aid to eligible students to help cover tuition costs.
> Overall, pursuing science after 10th grade can lead to a wide range of rewarding career opportunities with the potential for personal and professional growth.
