COMMERCE

Commerce courses after 10th offer a wide range of career options for students who have completed their 10th board exams. The commerce stream includes subjects like Economics, Accountancy, and Business Studies. Students can choose from Commerce with or without Mathematics and have the option to pursue various professional courses such as Chartered Accountancy, Company Secretary, Bachelor of Commerce, Bachelor of Business Administration, and more. Additionally, there are opportunities to pursue creative courses in arts, law, humanities, and related fields. Whether a student chooses mathematics or not, there are numerous career options available in the commerce stream.

Eligibility criteria: The eligibility criteria for commerce courses after 10th generally require candidates to have completed their 10th board exams from a recognized educational board. Additionally, some institutions may have specific requirements for admission to their commerce programs, such as minimum grades or specific subjects studied in 10th grade. It’s important to check the admission criteria of the specific institutions or courses you are interested in to ensure you meet their requirements.

Duration: The duration of commerce courses after 10th can vary depending on the specific program or degree. Typically, undergraduate commerce courses such as Bachelor of Commerce (BCom) or Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) are three-year programs. However, there are also specialized courses and professional degrees that may have different durations.
