PILOT

Information regarding becoming a pilot after completing 12th grade:

  • Eligibility Criteria: Must have completed 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Mathematics, and Chemistry. Minimum age requirement is usually 17-19 years, depending on the country and aviation authority. Must pass medical examinations conducted by aviation authorities.
  • Duration: The duration of pilot training programs varies, but it typically takes around 18-24 months to obtain a Commercial Pilot License (CPL).
  • Career Prospects: Career prospects for pilots can be promising, with opportunities to work for commercial airlines, private corporations, charter companies, cargo airlines, or governmental agencies. The demand for pilots fluctuates with economic conditions, but overall, there's a steady demand for qualified pilots globally.
  • Salary: Salaries for pilots vary widely depending on factors such as experience, type of aircraft flown, employer, and location. Entry-level pilots might start with salaries ranging from $30,000 to $60,000 annually, while experienced pilots flying for major airlines can earn six-figure salaries.
  • Entrance Exams: Some flight training academies or institutions may require candidates to pass entrance exams, which typically include tests on subjects like mathematics, physics, and English.
  • Course Fees: Course fees for pilot training can vary significantly based on factors such as the type of flight school, location, and type of aircraft used for training. Generally, flight training programs can cost anywhere from $50,000 to $150,000 or more for obtaining a CPL.
It's essential to research and choose a reputable flight training institution accredited by aviation authorities to ensure quality training and better career prospects.
